Parkinson's Disease Data Overview

Age Distribution

This pie chart represents the age distribution of patients diagnosed with Parkinson's disease. A significant proportion of cases occur in the 60-80 age group, but younger individuals can also be affected.

Symptom Severity

The radial chart illustrates the severity of key Parkinson's symptoms. Tremors and slowness of movement are commonly reported as more severe, followed by rigidity and balance issues.

Treatment Effectiveness

The bar chart shows the effectiveness of different treatments in managing Parkinson's disease symptoms. Medication tends to be the most effective, followed by exercise and therapy.
