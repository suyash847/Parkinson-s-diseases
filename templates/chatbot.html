What are the symptoms of Parkinson's Disease?
Common symptoms include tremors, stiffness, slow movement, and difficulty with balance.
What is the survival rate for Parkinson's Disease?
Approximately 80% of individuals with Parkinson's disease survive for more than 10 years after diagnosis.
What are the treatment options for Parkinson's Disease?
Treatments include medication like Levodopa, physical therapy, and sometimes surgery such as deep brain stimulation (DBS).
How does Parkinson's disease affect daily life?
Parkinson's disease can affect daily life by making simple tasks like walking, talking, and eating more difficult.
Which exercises are recommended for Parkinson's Disease?
Exercises like walking, swimming, and tai chi can help improve balance and mobility. Consult a physical therapist for a personalized exercise plan.
What precautions should a person with Parkinson's take?
Precautions include avoiding falls, staying physically active, managing stress, and regularly consulting with your doctor.
Is Parkinson's disease hereditary?
While most cases of Parkinson's are not hereditary, there is a genetic component in about 10-15% of cases.
Can Parkinson's disease cause cognitive decline?
Yes, Parkinson's disease can cause cognitive decline in some individuals, especially as the disease progresses.
What is deep brain stimulation (DBS)?
Deep brain stimulation (DBS) is a surgical treatment that involves implanting electrodes in the brain to reduce motor symptoms like tremors.
How can Parkinson's disease impact mental health?
Parkinson's can impact mental health by causing depression, anxiety, and sometimes apathy. Psychological support is important.
What is the role of diet in managing Parkinson's disease?
A healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and omega-3 fatty acids can help manage Parkinson's symptoms. Avoid processed foods and high sugar.
Can speech therapy help with Parkinson's disease?
Yes, speech therapy can help improve communication for people with Parkinson's who experience speech difficulties.
What are the early signs of Parkinson's disease?
Early signs of Parkinson's may include a slight tremor, changes in handwriting, loss of smell, and stiffness.
